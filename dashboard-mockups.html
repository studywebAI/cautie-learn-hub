New feature ideas
Things that don't exist yet — from codebase gaps + what teachers/students actually ask for
Missing submissions tracker
Teacher widget: "6 students haven't submitted the Biology essay." One click sends a reminder message to those students via class chat. Biggest Reddit complaint from teachers — always having to manually check who's missing.
quick win
Grade projection calculator
Student widget: "Your current avg is 87%. Score 80+ on the Biology test and you stay above 85%. Score below 65 and you drop to 79%." Very popular on r/highschool. Takes existing grade data and does the math forward.
medium lift
Quick attendance from dashboard
Teacher action item: mark attendance for today's class directly from the dashboard without navigating to the class tab. An inline "mark 3A attendance" row with present/absent counts. Saves 3+ clicks daily.
quick win
Catch-up mode
Student: when you're behind on a studyset, instead of showing all tasks, show "minimum viable plan — do these 3 things this week to catch up." Based on due dates and progress. Reddit students love this kind of scaffolding.
medium lift
Weekly digest notification
Monday morning: students get a card summarising last week (tasks completed, grades received, attendance) and what's coming this week. Teachers get class summary (avg grade, who's falling behind, what to grade). In-app first, optional email later.
medium lift
Study timer (Pomodoro)
Floating timer on the student dashboard. Start a 25-min focus session tied to a subject. At the end it marks progress on the studyset and logs study time to the analytics. Most requested feature on every study-app Reddit thread.
medium lift
Announcement banner
Teacher posts a short announcement to a class (test moved, no homework etc.) that shows as a pinned card at the top of the student dashboard for that class. Not buried in chat. The announcementManager component already exists — just needs surfacing on the dashboard.
quick win
Grade distribution chart
Teacher: after grading a quiz/essay, a small bar chart shows how grades spread across the class (how many A/B/C/fail). Teachers on Reddit say this is the first thing they want to see after marking. Already has analytics infrastructure.
quick win
How to use: Tell me which numbered designs you like — e.g. "student: S3 layout, T2 tone" or "mix S1 today-block with S5 grade projection." I can also build any of the new feature ideas. Which ones are interesting?
